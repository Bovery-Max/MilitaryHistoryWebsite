Honoring Service and Sacrifice

Throughout history, societies have created memorials, ceremonies, and traditions to remember those who served in the armed forces and to reflect on the costs of war.

War Memorials and Monuments

From simple grave markers to grand national monuments, memorials serve as focal points for remembrance and reflection.

Examples include: The Tomb of the Unknown Soldier (various countries), Vietnam Veterans Memorial (Washington DC), Thiepval Memorial (Somme, France), and the Australian War Memorial (Canberra).

Remembrance Days and Traditions

Special days set aside to honor military personnel who have died in service to their country.

Examples include: Remembrance Day/Veterans Day (November 11), Memorial Day (USA, last Monday in May), ANZAC Day (Australia/New Zealand, April 25), and various national independence or liberation day commemorations.

Learn About Traditions

Symbols of Remembrance

Particular objects, flowers, and gestures have become associated with military remembrance.

Examples include: The poppy (inspired by WWI poem "In Flanders Fields"), the bugle call "Last Post," moment of silence observances, memorial wreaths, and eternal flames at memorial sites.

Veterans' Stories and Oral Histories

Preserving firsthand accounts of military service provides invaluable historical records and personal connections to the past.

Projects include: Veterans History Project (Library of Congress), wartime diaries and letters collections, Holocaust survivor testimonies, and modern digital archives of service members' experiences.

Read Veterans' Stories

Military Cemeteries and Burial Traditions

Dedicated burial grounds for military personnel reflect societies' commitment to honoring their service.

Examples include: Arlington National Cemetery (USA), Normandy American Cemetery (France), Commonwealth War Graves Commission sites worldwide, and national war cemeteries in many countries.

Memorials to Peace and Reconciliation

Some memorials focus not only on loss but also on the hope for peace and reconciliation after conflict.

Examples include: Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park (Japan), the Peace Palace (The Hague), various reconciliation memorials in post-conflict societies, and monuments dedicated to peacekeeping efforts.
